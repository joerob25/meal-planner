04

The Principles

A quick primer on what the Longevity Diet actually is.

I

Mostly Plant-Based, Pescatarian

Legumes, vegetables, nuts, whole grains — with fish 2–3 times per week. No red meat. Minimal cheese and dairy. Low-mercury fish only: salmon, sardines, cod, trout, sea bream, prawns, anchovies, clams. Skip tuna and swordfish.

II

Low but Sufficient Protein

Under 65: roughly 0.7–0.8 g per kg of bodyweight, mostly from plants. After 65: slightly more, with fish, eggs, and white meat added to preserve muscle. Beans and chickpeas are your main protein source.

III

Complex Carbs & Good Fats

Whole grains, legumes, generous vegetables. About 3 tbsp of extra virgin olive oil daily and 30g (one handful) of nuts. Minimise sugar, saturated fat, refined flour.

IV

An 11–12 Hour Window

All meals within an 11–12 hour window — e.g. 8am to 8pm. Longo argues against tighter windows (8 hours or less) as harder to sustain and linked to gallstones and cardiovascular risk. Don't eat within 3–4 hours of bed.

V

Two or Three Meals — Not Six

If you're overweight: breakfast + one other meal + a low-sugar snack. If you're at a healthy weight, active, or over 65: three meals plus a snack. Never skip breakfast.

VI

The Fasting-Mimicking Diet

Two or three times per year, Longo recommends a 5-day fasting-mimicking protocol under medical supervision. This app doesn't generate the FMD — it's a specific clinical protocol — but it's the other half of the longevity equation.
